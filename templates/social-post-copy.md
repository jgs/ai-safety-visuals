## LinkedIn

### Alignment Objective Mismatch

AI alignment can be explained without hype.

A common failure pattern is simple: human intent gets compressed into a measurable proxy, and the system optimizes the proxy more directly than the thing people actually wanted.

This is why reward design, oversight, and evaluation matter.

Visual: `visuals/alignment/alignment-objective-mismatch.svg`
Sources: Amodei et al. 2016; Google DeepMind on specification gaming.

### Capability Vs Alignment

The safety question is not only whether models become more capable.

It is whether evaluations, safeguards, monitoring, and governance keep pace.

This visual is conceptual, not a plotted forecast. The point is the systems problem: capability changes create new evidence requirements.

Visual: `visuals/capability-vs-alignment/capability-vs-alignment-gap.svg`
Sources: OpenAI Preparedness Framework; Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy; METR.

### Task Automation Vs Job Replacement

AI labor-market debates get clearer when we separate task automation from full job replacement.

A job is a bundle of tasks. Some tasks may be automated, some augmented, and some remain human-led because of context, accountability, relationship work, or physical constraints.

Visual: `visuals/job-displacement/task-automation-vs-job-replacement.svg`
Sources: OECD AI and Work; Stanford AI Index.

## X

AI safety visuals should not pretend to measure what they only explain.

Rule I am using:
Conceptual diagram != chart.
Claim != evidence.
Exposure != replacement.
Benchmark != deployment.

Source registry first, visual second.

---

Task exposure is not the same claim as job replacement.

AI can automate parts of a role while leaving accountability, context, relationship work, and physical execution unresolved.

Better visuals make that distinction obvious.

---

Pre-deployment evaluation matters.
Post-deployment monitoring matters too.

The real world changes the input distribution, the user incentives, and the failure surface.

Evaluation should become an evidence loop, not a one-time gate.
