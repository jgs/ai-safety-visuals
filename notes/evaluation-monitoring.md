# Evaluation And Monitoring

Matching visual: [`evaluation-after-deployment.svg`](../visuals/evaluation/evaluation-after-deployment.svg)

## Core Idea

Pre-deployment evaluation is necessary but incomplete. Once deployed, AI systems encounter changing users, contexts, data distributions, and failure modes. Monitoring, incident review, red teaming, and feedback loops help keep evidence connected to real-world behavior.

## Source-Backed Claims

- NIST AI RMF frames AI risk management around governance, mapping, measuring, and managing risks. Source: `SRC-NIST-AI-RMF-1`.
- OpenAI's Preparedness Framework describes capability evaluations, safeguards, and governance around frontier risks. Source: `SRC-OPENAI-PREPAREDNESS-2025`.
- The International AI Safety Report discusses capabilities, risks, uncertainty, and the need for evidence-aware risk management. Source: `SRC-INTL-AI-SAFETY-REPORT-2025`.

## Claim Boundary

This visual is a process explainer, not a claim that monitoring eliminates risk. It also does not say a benchmark is useless; it says benchmark evidence should be complemented by deployment evidence.

## Social Caption

Benchmarks answer a narrow question: how did the system behave under a test setup? Deployment asks the harder question: what happens under changing users, incentives, data, and operating conditions?
