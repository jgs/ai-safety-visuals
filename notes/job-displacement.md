# Job Displacement And Task Exposure

Matching visual: [`task-automation-vs-job-replacement.svg`](../visuals/job-displacement/task-automation-vs-job-replacement.svg)

## Core Idea

AI can affect work at the task level before it replaces whole jobs. A role is usually a bundle of tasks: some may be automatable, some may be augmented, and some may remain human-led because they involve accountability, context, relationships, physical work, or institutional judgment.

## Source-Backed Claims

- OECD work on AI and labor uses occupational exposure and task-based framing to analyze how AI may affect jobs, skills, and work over time. Source: `SRC-OECD-AI-WORK`.
- Stanford AI Index reports broader AI adoption and ecosystem trends that can provide context when citing specific labor-market data. Source: `SRC-STANFORD-AI-INDEX`.

## Claim Boundary

This visual does not claim a percentage of jobs at risk. It does not equate task exposure with full job replacement. Any sector-specific or numeric claim must cite a specific report, year, and methodology.

## Social Caption

Better labor-market visuals separate task exposure from job replacement. AI may automate some tasks, augment others, and leave accountability-heavy work in human hands. Those are different claims, and they need different evidence.

Sources: OECD AI and Work; Stanford AI Index.
